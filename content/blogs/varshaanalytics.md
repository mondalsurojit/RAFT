Ask four sources how much it rained yesterday and you may get four answers. A rain gauge measures one exact point. Radar sees a wide sweep but infers depth indirectly. Satellites cover everywhere but at a coarser grain. Forecast models offer a fifth opinion about what's coming next. Each is right in its own way — and almost none of them sit in the same place, in the same units, on the same screen.

**VarshaAnalytics** is RAFT's rainfall-analytics product, built to close that gap: it brings station, radar, satellite and model rainfall together into a single, easy-to-read view.

## Why rainfall is hard to read

Rainfall is one of the most variable things in nature. It changes over a few kilometres and a few minutes, which is exactly the scale at which floods are decided. No single instrument captures it perfectly:

- **Stations** are accurate but sparse — a few points standing in for a whole city.
- **Radar** covers area and timing well, but estimates depth from reflectivity rather than measuring it.
- **Satellites** see everywhere, including ungauged terrain, but at a coarser resolution.
- **Models** extend the picture forward in time, with the uncertainty that any forecast carries.

Used in isolation, each can mislead. Used together, they corroborate one another — and the disagreement between them is itself a useful signal about confidence.

## One view, many sources

VarshaAnalytics' core job is fusion and legibility. It assembles those different streams into a unified view, and then does the harder thing: makes that view *readable* by people who are not hydrologists.

- **Rainfall monitoring** — a single, unified view of station, radar and satellite rainfall.
- **Extreme-rainfall insight** — detecting and contextualising heavy-rainfall events as they unfold.
- **Community dashboards** — accessible rainfall summaries written for non-experts.
- **Seasonal outlooks** — monsoon and seasonal rainfall perspectives for planning ahead.

## From a mascot to a product

VarshaAnalytics grew from **VarshaMitra** — IIT Hyderabad's 2023 community mascot (*Varsha* = rain, *Mitra* = friend) that championed crowd-sourced flood reporting for SnapFlood. What began as a friendly face for community engagement matured into a tool that does the analytical work behind it: turning the rainfall record into something a community or a decision-maker can actually act on.

## Why legibility is the point

A forecast that only an expert can read helps only experts. The value of rainfall intelligence is realised at the moment a resident, a city engineer or a planner understands what the numbers mean for *them* — and decides what to do next. That is the bar VarshaAnalytics is built to clear.
